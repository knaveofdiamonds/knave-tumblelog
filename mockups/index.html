Sliced sign up

Merb slices have landed, hopefully bringing a cleaner version of rails engines to the other ruby framework. Still only on edge/0.9.4 merb for now.

Authentication is an obvious target for slices, and there are several implementations - can_has_auth which I failed to get working (merb moves so quickly that things break) and merb-auth, amongst others. The problem is that like most authentication plugins/extensions/whatevers, there is little flexibility in the workflow of the sign up process, and that default workflow isn’t necessarily the best.

I wish more apps followed the model used by Gravatar and Launchpad, where you just have to give an email address to sign up: adding a password, username or whatever other account details are needed is wrapped up in the ‘post-email-address-validation’ stage.

This works especially well if you’ve made the decision to allow people to use your application before registering - the registration step becomes a “I need to save my stuff” rather than a “I want to try this out” goal. With this mindset a user is going to get more frustrated by barriers - they can’t just walk away.

Looking at the examples, creating slices doesn’t seem particularly tricky, so packaging another authentication workflow is possible. The problem is that everyone is going to want a slightly different way of doing things, and then a hundred authentication slices are born.

The real value might just come from being able to reuse slices in your own projects, rather than feeling you have to pick up something generic and tweak it - keep them open so others can learn from them and get reuse for yourself.
